Thesis Proposal Examination

Critical Timeline: You must pass your proposal exam within two semesters of your first thesis registration. Failure to do so may result in a delayed graduation timeline.
1. Form Submission (GR39)

Submit the GR39 form via the e-Form system at least 15 days before the exam date.

e-Form Portal e-Form Submission Guide
2. Committee Composition

Number of committee members:

• For MSc Students: At least 2 members
• For PhD Students: At least 3 members

Committee Qualifications

  • Mahidol Faculty: Have at least 3 academic works in past 5 years (including at least one research work).
  • New Mahidol Faculty: Have at least 1 academic work in past 2 years OR 2 in past 4 years (excluding publications completed as part of their doctoral studies).
  • Outside Mahidol: Have at least 5 publications in the international databases (excluding publications completed as part of their doctoral studies).
3. After Approval & Result Submission
  • Download the GR33 form (Evaluation of the thesis proposal exam) from the e-Form system and prepare one copy for the exam day.
  • Submit the signed GR33 form at the departmental office.
  • If result is "Pass with conditions": Revise your thesis proposal according to the suggestions received from the committee, then download the GR37 form and submit the signed GR37 form at the departmental office within 90 days from the exam date.
1. Form Submission (GRAS39)

Submit the GRAS39 form via the e-Form system at least 15 days before the exam date.

e-Form Portal e-Form Submission Guide
2. Committee Composition

Number of committee members:

• For MSc Students: At least 2 and up to 3 members
• For PhD Students: At least 3 and up to 5 members

Committee Qualifications

  • Mahidol Faculty: Have at least 3 academic works in past 5 years (including at least one research work).
  • New Mahidol Faculty: Have at least 1 academic work in past 2 years OR 2 in past 4 years (excluding publications completed as part of their doctoral studies).
  • Outside Mahidol: Have at least 5 publications in the international databases (excluding publications completed as part of their doctoral studies).
3. After Approval & Result Submission
No result submission required: Students do not need to submit the result of their thesis proposal exam. Once the exam is completed, please proceed directly to the Thesis Title Approval and Advisory Committee Appointing (GR1) process.
